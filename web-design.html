02

Built Right Underneath

Two sites can look identical and perform nothing alike. The difference is under the hood — and it's standard in every site we ship, not an upsell.

🔍

Technical SEO foundations

Clean metadata, structured data, sitemaps, and a logical heading hierarchy on every page — the groundwork search engines read before they decide whether to show you. Skipping it is why pretty sites stay invisible.

📱

Mobile-first usability

Most local searches happen on a phone, often in a driveway or a parking lot. We design for that screen first: readable text, tappable buttons, and a phone number that's never more than a thumb away.

⚡

Performance

No bloated themes, no plugin stacks, no three-megabyte hero video. Lean pages that load fast on a cellular connection — because that's where your customer is when they decide whether to wait or hit back. See how we cut our own homepage from 204 MB to 9.7 MB in this case study.

♿

Accessibility

Keyboard navigation, real color contrast, reduced-motion support, and labels screen readers can use. It's the right way to build, it widens who can actually use your site, and it overlaps heavily with what search engines reward.

🗂️

Content structure that answers searches

Pages organized around what visitors actually type: the service, the area, the price question, the "is this worth it" question. When the site answers what people search, rankings and calls tend to follow.

03

Custom Code vs. CMS — an Honest Answer

Plenty of shops push whatever platform they resell. We recommend per case, not per our convenience — and the deciding question is simple: how often will you actually edit the site?

🧱

Custom-coded static sites

Fast, secure, and low-maintenance: no plugins to update, no database to hack, hosting that costs next to nothing. For most local-business marketing sites — where content changes a few times a year — this is the right call, and we handle the occasional edits.

📝

CMS-based sites

Right when you genuinely edit content weekly — blog posts, menus, listings, events — and want to do it yourself without calling anyone. The trade-off is real: more upkeep, more moving parts, and more that can slow down or break. Worth it when you'll use it; waste when you won't.

🤝

Our recommendation, in writing

During discovery we ask how your content actually changes — not how you hope it might — and recommend accordingly. Whichever way it goes, the reasoning is in your written scope, so you're choosing with the trade-offs on the table.
