What We Automate

The repetitive, rule-following work that eats hours and invites typos. Not sure which of your processes qualify? Read our guide: what should you automate?

Talk Through a Process →
🧭

Lead routing & assignment

For the lead that sat in a shared inbox all weekend. New inquiries get routed to the right person by territory, service, or availability — the moment they arrive, not when someone checks.

✅

Approval workflows

For the purchase order stuck under a stack on someone's desk. Requests route to the right approver, remind them if they stall, and escalate if they sit — with a record of who approved what and when.

🚨

Notifications & escalations

For the problem nobody heard about until it was expensive. When a job stalls, a payment fails, or a deadline nears, the right person gets told — and if they don't act, the next person up does.

📅

Scheduling & reminders

For the no-shows and the calendar built by phone tag. Bookings, confirmations, and reminder texts run on their own, and reschedules update everyone who needs to know.

📄

Document generation

For the quote that takes forty-five minutes of copy-paste. Quotes, invoices, and contracts fill from templates using data you already have — consistent, correct, and out the door in minutes.

⌨️

Data entry & re-keying

For the same customer typed into three systems by three people. Data entered once flows to everywhere it belongs — no re-keying, no transposed digits, no "which one is right?"

🤝

CRM handoffs

For the deal that closed in sales and vanished on the way to operations. When a stage changes in your CRM, the next team gets the job, the details, and the paperwork — automatically.

✉️

Operational email & text sequences

For the updates your team means to send but doesn't. Job started, job done, invoice sent, payment overdue — messages triggered by what actually happened in your operations, not by someone remembering.

🔌

Integrations (APIs & webhooks)

For the two systems that both matter and don't talk. We connect your tools directly through their APIs and webhooks, so the connection is yours — not rented from a middleman forever, subject to third-party platform terms and the written agreement.

02

Built to Not Break

The difference between automation that helps and automation that quietly wrecks your data is what happens on the bad days. We design for the bad days.

🧑‍⚖️

Human review where judgment matters

Not every step should run on its own. Where a call requires judgment — an unusual discount, a borderline approval, a sensitive customer — the workflow pauses and asks a person. Automation moves the paper; people make the calls.

🛟

Failure handling & alerts

When an automation errs — an API is down, a record is malformed — it stops safely, keeps the failed item, and alerts someone with enough detail to fix it. The worst automations fail silently for weeks. Ours don't.

🧾

Audit trails

Every run is logged: who or what triggered it, what it changed, and when. When someone asks "why did this invoice go out?", there's an answer you can look up instead of a shrug.

⛔

What should NOT be automated

Plainly: judgment calls, exception-heavy processes, and anything that's expensive when it's wrong. If half your cases are special cases, automation will make the mess faster, not better. We'll tell you when a process isn't ready — fixing the process comes first.

03

Process & Ownership

Automating a process you haven't mapped just automates the confusion. We do it in order — and what we build belongs to you.

Start With Discovery →
🗺️

Map the current process first

We sit with the people who do the work and chart how it actually flows — including the workarounds nobody wrote down. This is where the real requirements surface, and where broken steps get fixed before they get automated.

🧱

Automate the stable parts

We start with the steps that are consistent and rule-following — the ones automation is good at — and leave the volatile parts with people until they settle. Better a smaller automation that's trusted than a big one that's babysat.

📈

Monitor and adjust

After launch we watch the runs with you: what's flowing, what's erroring, what the team actually uses. Workflows get tuned based on real behavior, not the plan on paper.

🔑

You own it all

The workflows, the accounts they run on, and the documentation explaining how each one works — all in your name. If we disappeared tomorrow, your automations would keep running and the next developer could read exactly how.

🤝

Support plan optional

Keep us on a monthly plan to monitor, fix, and extend your workflows — or take the documented hand-off and run them in-house. Both are real options; neither is a trap.
