02

Is Custom Software Right for You?

Honestly — not always. Custom is a real investment, and we'd rather tell you no than build something you didn't need. For the full decision framework, read our custom software vs. off-the-shelf guide.

✅

When custom is the right call

  • Your process is your competitive edge, and generic software flattens it.
  • Off-the-shelf tools force workarounds — you run your business around the software instead of the other way around.
  • Your team re-keys the same data between tools every day.
  • You've outgrown the spreadsheets: too many rows, too many editors, no accountability.
⛔

When it isn't

  • A solid off-the-shelf product already matches how you work — buy it, don't build it.
  • Your budget is under a few thousand dollars. Custom work below that mark cuts corners you'll pay for later.
  • You need it live next week. Good custom software takes discovery and staged building; a rush job isn't worth owning.

How a Project Runs

Scoped in writing, priced before the build, and visible the whole way through.

Start With Discovery →
🧭

Discovery

We sit down with the people who actually do the work and map the current process — the tools, the handoffs, the workarounds. This is where most of the real requirements surface.

📝

Written scope & written quote

You get a document that says exactly what will be built, what it will cost, and when it will land. No hourly meter running while you hope for the best.

🧱

Build in stages you can see

We ship working pieces as we go and put them in front of you early, so course corrections happen when they're cheap — not after the reveal.

🚀

Launch with training

We move your data in, train your team on the real system, and stay close through the first weeks when questions actually come up.

🔧

Support

After launch you pick the arrangement that fits: a monthly support plan, or a documented hand-off. Software needs upkeep either way — we just make sure it's your choice who does it.

⚖️

What moves cost and timeline

Four things, mostly: how many systems we integrate with, how much existing data has to migrate, how many user roles and permission levels you need, and how deep the reporting goes. Discovery pins these down before you commit.

What You Own

Everything. Plenty of shops keep the keys — the code, the hosting login, the "proprietary platform" you can never leave. We don't. When the project ends, it's yours, and that's in the agreement.

💾

Source code

The full codebase, in a repository under your account. No license fees, no lock-in, and any competent developer can pick it up after us.

🗃️

Your data

Stored in standard formats you can export any time. Your customer list and job history are business assets — they never live somewhere you can't reach.

📄

Documentation

What the system does, how it's put together, and how to run it — written for the next person, whether that's your staff or another developer.

🔑

Accounts

Hosting, domain, database, and third-party services registered in your name from day one — not ours. If we disappeared tomorrow, you'd lose nothing.

🤝

Maintenance, your way

A monthly support plan where we handle updates, fixes, and small changes — or a clean hand-off with documentation and full access. Both are real options; neither is a trap.
