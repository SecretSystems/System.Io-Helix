02

The Details That Make It Useful

The difference between a dashboard people use daily and one they abandon is in the plumbing — the parts you don't see on the screen.

Data-Source Integration

We connect your CRM, spreadsheets, accounting tools in the QuickBooks class, and databases. If a tool has an export or an API, we can usually pull from it — and we'll confirm each source during discovery, not after the contract.

Data Quality First

A dashboard is only as good as its inputs. Before we build a single chart, we clean up definitions — what counts as a sale, an active job, a closed lead — so the numbers on screen are ones everyone trusts.

Scheduled vs. Near-Real-Time Refresh

An honest trade-off: nightly or hourly refreshes are cheaper to build and run, and they're enough for most decisions. Near-real-time costs more and earns it only for things like inbound leads or live job status. We'll tell you which parts need which.

Permissions & User Roles

The owner sees financials; crew leads see their jobs; the front desk sees schedules. Each person gets the view their role needs — nothing more, nothing hidden that they should have.

Exports & Scheduled Reports

Download any view as a spreadsheet or PDF, or have a summary land in the right inboxes every Monday morning — automatically, without anyone remembering to send it.

Alerts & Thresholds

Set a line, and get an email or text when a number crosses it — a job past its due date, cash below a floor, a lead untouched for 48 hours. The dashboard watches so you don't have to.

Mobile Access

Readable on a phone in a truck, not just on a desktop in the office. The views that matter in the field are designed for the field.

03

Process & Ownership

Built in the open, documented as we go, and yours when it's done.

Discovery First

We start by asking which decisions the dashboard serves, who looks at it, and how often. A dashboard nobody checks is a decoration — so we design around real habits, not hypothetical ones.

Metric Definitions in Writing

Before the build starts, we agree in writing on what each number means and where it comes from. When the dashboard says revenue, everyone knows exactly what's being counted.

Build, Then Training

We build against the agreed definitions, then walk your team through it — what each view shows, how to filter, and how to trust-check a number against the source system.

You Own It

The code, the configuration, and the data are yours. Maintain it in-house, hand it to another developer, or add an optional maintenance plan — your call, and never a requirement.
